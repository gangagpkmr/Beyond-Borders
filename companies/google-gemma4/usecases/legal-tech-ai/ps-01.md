# PS-01: Legal Document Explainer for Self-Represented Individuals

> **Company:** Google Gemma 4
> **Vertical:** Legal Tech AI
> **Technology:** Gemma 4 On-Device (12B) + Long Context (256K)

---

## Problem Statement

In most legal systems, the ability to understand and respond to legal documents is the difference between justice and injustice. Summonses, court orders, motions, plea agreements, eviction orders, bail conditions, and debt judgments are written in a specialized legal register that is opaque to the vast majority of people - including educated, literate adults.

Missing a response deadline because you did not understand it was one can result in a default judgment against you. Signing a plea agreement without understanding what rights you are waiving can have consequences that last decades. Failing to respond to an eviction notice in the correct format can result in homelessness - not because you had no case, but because you did not know how the system worked.

Legal aid organizations serve fewer than 20% of eligible low-income individuals in most countries. In India, the ratio of lawyers to population is approximately 1:1,000 in urban areas and far worse in rural regions. The justice gap is not primarily a problem of law - it is a problem of access and comprehension.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Unrepresented defendants in criminal, civil, family, and housing courts
- Tenants facing eviction proceedings without legal representation
- Individuals navigating debt recovery, consumer disputes, or labor tribunals
- Domestic violence survivors dealing with protection orders and custody paperwork
- Legal aid clinics and court self-help centers overwhelmed by intake volume
- First-generation immigrants navigating legal systems in a non-native language

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- The consequences of legal incomprehension are irreversible in ways that most other information gaps are not. A default judgment, a wrongful guilty plea, a missed custody hearing, a waived right to appeal - these cannot be easily undone. The asymmetry between legally represented and unrepresented parties in the same proceeding is one of the most acute forms of structural inequality in modern society.
- Privacy is a compounding constraint: legal documents contain the most sensitive personal information that exists - criminal records, financial disclosures, child custody details, domestic abuse history. Cloud-based AI tools are categorically inappropriate for this context. An on-device solution is not a preference - it is a prerequisite for ethical deployment.

---

## Proposed Solution

A Gemma 4 12B-powered legal document explainer running entirely on-device at legal aid clinics, court self-help centers, community organizations, and on personal devices. A person uploads or photographs a legal document. The system identifies the document type, explains what it is, what it requires the person to do, by when, and what happens if they do not act - in plain language, in their language.

**Key features:**
- Identifies document type (summons, judgment, motion, eviction notice, plea agreement, etc.)
- Extracts and explains: what this document means, what action is required, deadline, consequences of inaction
- Flags clauses that the person should specifically ask a lawyer about before signing
- Explains legal terminology in plain language with examples
- Supports 140+ languages - critical for immigrant and minority language communities
- 256K context window handles full case files and multi-document sets in a single session
- Zero data transmission - all processing on-device, no cloud, no logging

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- **Huge Context Window:** The 256K context window is decisive for legal use cases. A full case file - complaint, responses, motions, orders - can run to hundreds of pages. Gemma 4 12B can hold an entire case history in context and answer questions about it coherently, without losing earlier information as the conversation continues.
- **Strict Data Confidentiality:** On-device deployment is not optional in this domain. Legal documents are among the most sensitive data a person holds. A tool that requires uploading legal documents to a third-party server creates ethical, legal, and reputational risk for any organization deploying it. Gemma 4 running locally eliminates this entirely.
- **Instant Response Times:** Local inference provides zero network latency, allowing users to parse documents in real time at intake desks.

---

## Desirability

The demand is urgent and unmet. Court self-help centers report turning away dozens of people per day who simply need someone to explain a document to them. Legal aid intake workers spend the majority of their time on explanation tasks that could be handled by a well-designed tool - freeing them for tasks that genuinely require legal expertise.

**Desirability Score:** 10/10

---

## Feasibility

Legal document classification and plain-language explanation are tasks Gemma 4 12B handles reliably. The primary engineering challenges are: (1) building a robust document type taxonomy and prompt library for major legal document categories; (2) jurisdiction-specific calibration (Indian legal system differs from UK common law differs from US federal system); and (3) designing the UI for low-digital-literacy users at court self-help centers.

**Feasibility Score:** 8/10

## Possible MVP

A laptop application running Gemma 4 12B deployed at 5 court self-help centers across 2 states. Covers 8 document types: eviction notice, summons, bail order, consumer court notice, maintenance order, debt recovery notice, land acquisition notice, and motor accident tribunal notice. Tested over 4 months with self-help center staff. Success metric: reduction in average staff time per intake, and participant comprehension test scores.

**Timeline:** 10 weeks to working prototype, 5 months to court pilot.

---

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Model gives incorrect legal interpretation with confidence | Medium | Consistent disclaimer that output is explanation not legal advice; always recommend lawyer review for action steps |
| Jurisdiction variation makes general prompts unreliable | High | Build jurisdiction-specific prompt libraries; start with one state legal system |
| Users treat output as legal advice and act without counsel | Medium | UI design: clear 'explain, not advise' framing at every step; escalation path to human |
| Data privacy in shared device environments at clinics | Low | Session wipe after each use; no local logging; no document storage |
