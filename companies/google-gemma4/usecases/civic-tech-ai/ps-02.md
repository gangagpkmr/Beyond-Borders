# PS-02: Policy & Legislation Plain-Language Converter

> **Company:** Google Gemma 4
> **Vertical:** Civic Tech AI
> **Technology:** Gemma 4 On-Device (12B) + Multilingual + Function Calling

---

## Problem Statement

Democratic governance depends on an informed citizenry. The legal infrastructure of democracy - bills, ordinances, budget documents, municipal by-laws, policy circulars - is, by construction, written for legal compliance rather than public comprehension. This is not an accident of history; it is the default output of legislative and administrative drafting processes that have never prioritized readability.

Public consultations on new legislation, local development plans, and annual budgets legally invite citizen input - but the documents that define these processes are written in bureaucratic and legal language that the overwhelming majority of the population cannot meaningfully engage with. The result is that participation in formal democratic processes is structurally dominated by organized interest groups, legal and policy professionals, and well-funded lobbies - not the general public.

In multilingual societies - India with 22 scheduled languages, South Africa with 11 official languages, the Philippines, Indonesia - the problem compounds further. Official legislative documents are produced in one or two languages of power. Communities that participate in civic life primarily in other languages are effectively excluded from governance processes that affect them.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Citizens invited to comment on draft legislation, municipal plans, or budget proposals
- Civil society organizations preparing public submissions on policy documents
- Regional language communities excluded from English or Hindi-only legislative processes
- Journalists covering policy who need to translate legislation into reporting that audiences understand
- Civic education organizations working to increase democratic participation
- Municipal governments seeking to make public consultation more than a procedural formality

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- The quality of democratic governance is directly proportional to the quality of public participation. When the people most affected by a policy cannot understand it, their interests go unrepresented in the design of that policy. Laws that affect agricultural communities are shaped by urban lawyers. Budgets that determine healthcare access are written in financial language that healthcare workers cannot parse. Local development plans that reshape neighborhoods go unchallenged because residents could not understand the planning documents.
- This is not a marginal problem. It is a structural deficit at the heart of representative democracy - one that technology, for the first time, has the tools to address at scale and at cost.

---

## Proposed Solution

A Gemma 4-powered civic document translation platform deployable by civic tech organizations, municipal governments, and civil society groups. Policy and legislative documents are uploaded (or fetched via URL through function calling). Gemma 4 12B processes the document and produces a layered plain-language output: a 3-sentence summary for general public, a structured section-by-section explainer for engaged citizens, and a list of participation-ready questions for formal submissions.

**Key features:**
- Processes legislation, budget documents, policy circulars, municipal plans, and government reports
- Produces tiered output: headline summary, section-by-section explainer, key questions for public comment
- Translates output into 140+ languages - enabling communities to engage in their preferred language
- Function calling integration to fetch documents from government portals automatically
- Identifies who benefits and who bears costs in policy documents - surfaces distributional impacts
- Generates shareable civic briefs that organizations can distribute to their communities
- Deployable offline for organizations in low-connectivity areas, or as a web service for higher-connectivity contexts

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- **Language Advantage at the Edge:** Multilingual capability across 140+ languages is the differentiating feature for this use case. No other open model at this size delivers reliable output quality across the range of Indian languages, Southeast Asian languages, and African languages needed for genuinely inclusive civic engagement.
- **Agentic Automation:** Function calling enables agentic workflows: the system can fetch documents from government URLs, check for document updates, and trigger new analysis automatically when a new draft is published - keeping civic organizations informed without manual monitoring.
- **Large Context Capacity:** The 256K context window handles multi-part legislative bills and full budget documents in a single local session.
- **Low Operational Cost:** Deploying the model locally allows local organizations to summarize and translate thousands of pages without incurring ongoing cloud API token fees.

---

## Desirability

Civic tech organizations, bar associations, and NGO coalitions consistently identify 'making policy accessible' as a top priority and a chronic capacity gap. Municipal governments that run legally mandated consultations often receive zero substantive public comments - not from apathy, but because the documents are incomprehensible. A tool that demonstrably increases the quality and volume of public participation is desirable to every stakeholder in democratic governance.

**Desirability Score:** 9/10

---

## Feasibility

Document summarization, plain-language conversion, and multilingual output are core Gemma 4 capabilities. The agentic document-fetching via function calling is supported natively. The primary development challenges are: (1) building a civic document taxonomy and structured output schema across government document types; (2) calibrating 'distributional impact' identification for different policy domains; and (3) designing a shareable brief format that civic organizations can actually use in community outreach.

**Feasibility Score:** 8/10

## Possible MVP

A web application running Gemma 4 12B, tested with 6 civil society organizations across 2 states. Processes 3 document types: state budget documents, draft municipal development plans, and state legislative bills. Produces tiered plain-language output in English + 3 regional languages. Evaluated by civic educators and public policy experts on accuracy, accessibility, and usefulness for community mobilization.

**Timeline:** 8 weeks to working prototype, 4 months to civil society pilot.

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Model oversimplifies nuanced policy trade-offs | Medium | Tiered output design: headline for general public, full analysis for policy-engaged users |
| Government resistance to citizen tools that enable scrutiny | Low-Medium | Frame as civic participation infrastructure, not opposition tool; seek government co-deployment |
| Translation quality in lower-resource regional languages | Medium | Quality validation with regional language experts; start with 10 highest-coverage languages |
| Document format variation across government portals | Medium | Build format-agnostic ingestion pipeline; fallback to manual upload for non-standard formats |
