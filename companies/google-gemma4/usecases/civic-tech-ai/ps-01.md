# PS-01: Low-Literacy Document Simplifier for Vulnerable Communities

> **Company:** Google Gemma 4
> **Vertical:** Civic Tech AI
> **Technology:** Gemma 4 On-Device (E2B/12B) + OCR

---

## Problem Statement

Critical life documents - eviction notices, utility disconnection warnings, school enrollment deadlines, government benefit forms, medical discharge instructions - are routinely written at a 12th-grade reading level. The average adult in India reads at a 6th-grade level, and significant portions of the population are functionally illiterate in the official language of governance.

The people most impacted by these documents - low-income families, recent migrants, elderly citizens, first-generation urban workers - are precisely those least equipped to parse them. They miss deadlines, sign agreements they do not understand, forfeit entitlements, or pay fines for violations they were unaware of.

Frontline NGO workers and caseworkers who support these populations cannot manually simplify every document for every client. A caseworker serving 80+ individuals per week lacks the bandwidth to rewrite letters in plain language - and when clients speak different languages, the problem compounds further.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Low-income urban and rural families receiving government notices and welfare communications
- Migrants and refugees navigating housing, legal, and healthcare paperwork in a non-native language
- Elderly citizens managing pension, healthcare, and utility documents without family support
- NGO caseworkers and social workers serving high volumes of clients with diverse literacy levels
- Adult literacy programs seeking tools to bridge the gap between document complexity and learner capability

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- Inaccessible documents are not a minor inconvenience - they are a structural barrier that compounds poverty. A missed benefit enrollment window can cost a family months of welfare support. An unread eviction notice can result in a family losing housing without understanding they had a right to contest. A misread medical discharge form can lead to avoidable readmission.
- The problem is systemic: governments draft documents for legal compliance, not comprehension. There is no incentive in the current system to simplify - and no affordable mechanism to do so at scale. An on-device AI solution changes this equation fundamentally.

---

## Proposed Solution

A Gemma 4-powered document simplification tool deployable on low-cost Android tablets or laptops at NGO offices, libraries, community centers, and field outreach stations. A caseworker or the document recipient photographs or uploads any official document. Gemma 4 (running entirely on-device with no internet required) performs OCR, identifies the document type, and rewrites the content in plain language at a specified reading level - in the user's preferred language.

**Key features:**
- Reads printed and handwritten documents via Gemma 4 vision (variable resolution OCR)
- Rewrites content at grade 3–5 reading level in the recipient's language
- Highlights key deadlines, required actions, and consequences of inaction
- Supports 140+ languages including regional Indian languages (Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Marathi, Bengali, etc.)
- Zero internet dependency - runs fully offline on E2B or 12B model
- Audio output option for non-literate users

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- **Privacy & Data Security:** Documents contain highly sensitive personal data - names, addresses, financial details, health records. Cloud-based tools create data liability for NGOs and expose vulnerable individuals to data misuse. Gemma 4 on-device ensures the document never leaves the device.
- **Offline Reliability:** Many field offices and community centers operate in remote or low-connectivity environments. An internet-dependent system would fail when needed most.
- **Compute Efficiency:** The Gemma 4 E2B model runs under 1.5GB RAM on standard Android hardware, making it deployable on existing community center tablets without hardware procurement.

---

## Desirability

Caseworkers spend significant uncompensated time explaining documents verbally. A tool that produces a plain-language version in 10 seconds changes their workflow fundamentally. Document recipients gain agency - they can understand and act on communications without depending on intermediaries.

**Desirability Score:** 9/10

---

## Feasibility

Gemma 4's vision model handles OCR and document parsing natively. Plain-language rewriting is a well-understood task that the 12B model performs reliably. The multilingual output is already built in. The primary engineering challenge is fine-tuning reading level calibration for specific document categories (legal, medical, government) and building a lightweight wrapper UI.

**Feasibility Score:** 8/10

---

## Possible MVP

A tablet application running Gemma 4 E2B deployed at 10 NGO field offices across 2 cities. Trained on 5 document categories: eviction notices, utility bills, benefit enrollment forms, school admission letters, and medical discharge summaries. Tested with 200 caseworkers over 3 months. Success metric: document comprehension test score improvement in end recipients.

**Timeline:** 8 weeks to working prototype, 4 months to pilot deployment.

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Over-simplification omits critical legal detail | Medium | Dual output: plain + original side-by-side; legal review of output templates |
| OCR accuracy on low-quality printed documents | Medium | Fine-tune vision model on Indian document styles; fallback to manual text entry |
| Language coverage gaps in regional dialects | Low-Medium | Partner with regional language NLP groups; start with 10 core languages |
| NGO adoption and device availability | Low | Partner with existing tablet lending programs; E2B fits on basic Android hardware |
