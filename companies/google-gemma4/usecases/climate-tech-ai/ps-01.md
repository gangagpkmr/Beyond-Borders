# PS-01: Environmental Report Analyst for Community Groups

> **Company:** Google Gemma 4
> **Vertical:** Climate Tech AI
> **Technology:** Gemma 4 On-Device (12B/27B) + 256K Context

---

## Problem Statement

Environmental Impact Assessments (EIAs) are legally mandated documents that governments require before approving major industrial projects - mines, dams, factories, highways, thermal plants. In India and most of Asia, they serve as the primary mechanism through which affected communities have a formal right to challenge a project.

In practice, this right is hollow. EIAs routinely run 500–2,000 pages of dense scientific, regulatory, and engineering language. Affected communities have a legally mandated public comment window - often 30 days - but the documents are practically unreadable without environmental science, hydrology, or legal expertise. Critical risks to air quality, water supply, and agricultural land are buried in appendices. Consequences for non-compliance are written in regulatory cross-references that require a specialist to trace.

Hiring an environmental consultant to review a full EIA costs ₹3–10 lakh - far beyond the reach of a village panchayat, a watershed protection collective, or a tribal land rights group. As a result, most EIA public comment periods produce no substantive community input. Projects proceed with adverse impacts that were technically disclosed but practically invisible.

---

## Who Faces This Problem

- Tribal communities and gram sabhas facing mining or dam projects on ancestral land
- Farmers dependent on river systems threatened by industrial water extraction
- Grassroots environmental NGOs without technical staff or consultant budgets
- Journalists investigating environmental governance without scientific backgrounds
- Panchayat officials legally required to hold public hearings on EIAs without capacity to understand them

---

## Why the Problem Matters

- India loses approximately 1.5 million hectares of forest cover annually, significantly driven by industrial projects that receive EIA clearance with no meaningful public challenge. River systems serving hundreds of millions of people face industrial-scale water extraction that communities cannot contest because they cannot parse the technical documents that disclose it.
- The asymmetry is structural: corporations and governments have teams of environmental consultants who write EIAs; communities have 30 days and no resources. Gemma 4 can close this gap - not by replacing expert judgment, but by making the document comprehensible enough for affected communities to know what questions to ask and what to challenge.

---

## Proposed Solution

A Gemma 4 12B-powered EIA analysis tool running on-device at NGO offices, panchayat offices, or on a standard laptop brought to village meetings. A user uploads the EIA document (PDF, often 500–1,500 pages). The system - using Gemma 4's 256K context window - processes the full document and produces a structured community briefing that surfaces what matters.

**Key features:**
- Ingests full EIA PDFs using Gemma 4 vision and long-context processing
- Produces a structured community briefing: project summary, affected areas, key risks (air, water, land, livelihoods), commitments made by the proponent, and gaps/omissions
- Flags sections that contradict each other or omit legally required disclosures
- Extracts all numerical claims (pollution levels, water usage, displacement figures) for independent verification
- Generates a set of public comment questions the community can formally submit
- Outputs in regional language - critical for communities whose primary language is not English or Hindi
- Fully offline - EIA documents may contain commercially sensitive data that NGOs are prohibited from sharing externally

---

## Why Edge AI Is Needed

- **Massive Local Context:** The 256K context window is the decisive capability here. A 1,200-page EIA cannot be processed in chunks without losing cross-document coherence - contradictions between the executive summary and the technical annexures are a common strategy for obscuring adverse impacts, and detecting them requires holding the full document in context simultaneously.
- **Scanned PDF Parsing:** The vision model handles scanned PDFs - many EIAs are submitted as image scans to discourage text search.
- **Data Privacy & Security:** On-device deployment means a tribal community NGO does not need to upload a commercially sensitive land-use document to a third-party cloud. Gemma 4 running on a ₹50,000 laptop provides capabilities that previously required a ₹5 lakh consultant engagement.

---

## Desirability

Environmental NGOs report that lack of technical capacity to engage with EIAs is their single largest operational constraint. Village communities legally entitled to participate in public hearings routinely attend without understanding the document that is the subject of the hearing. A tool that produces a 5-page plain-language briefing from a 1,000-page EIA is not a convenience - it is an enabler of rights that are currently theoretical.

**Desirability Score:** 9/10

---

## Feasibility

Long-document summarization and structured information extraction are tasks where Gemma 4 12B performs reliably. The primary challenge is building a domain-specific prompt library calibrated to Indian EIA format requirements under the Environment Protection Act and EIA Notification 2006, so outputs are legally relevant rather than generically summarized.

**Feasibility Score:** 8/10

## Viability

**Revenue models:**

---

## Possible MVP

A laptop application running Gemma 4 12B, tested with 8 environmental NGOs across 3 active EIA cases in mining and dam sectors. Produces: (1) a 5-page community briefing, (2) a list of 10 substantive public comment questions, and (3) a flagged list of numerical claims for verification. Evaluated by environmental lawyers for accuracy and coverage over a 3-month pilot.

**Timeline:** 10 weeks to working prototype, 5 months to NGO pilot.

## Risks and Challenges

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Model misses critical technical disclosures in specialist sections | Medium | Structured prompt coverage of all legally required EIA sections; expert review in pilot |
| Very large scanned PDFs exceed processing capacity | Medium | Prioritized section processing with user-directed deep dives; 256K context handles most cases |
| Legal interpretation errors in community briefings | Medium | Consistent framing as 'questions to investigate' not 'legal findings'; partner NGO review step |
| Government or corporate pressure on NGO partners | Low | On-device model cannot be remotely accessed or shut down; open-source deployment option |
